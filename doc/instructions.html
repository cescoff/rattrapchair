Laser Cut

Before launching you laser cut

Here is a list of tools you need

  • 8mm span plywood (enough to do your chairs). The span MUST be 8mm
  • A pencil
  • A box that you can maintain easily closed (for some tiny parts)
  • Tiny elastics to keep some parts together and not lose them (if you are making more than 1 chair)

Dealing with the files

The files are in SVG format. This is an open source format supported by most of the vector drawing softwares. All you have to do is right click on the file and choose "Open with" and then open the file within your favorite installed software

How to handle the wood once cut

Note: all layers are cut with the top face of the chair on the top of the laser cut plan. The layers are not really oriented but at least it is recommended to keep this orientation

Layer A

The image below shows you what pieces of wood are wasted on this layer. All striped areas in red are the one that you actually can drop

Once all wasted wood parts are removed you can flip the parts and write the layer name on the back of them. Parts from different layers look the same and should not be swapped at mount time.

If you are printing a chair in split mode, please note to which part of the split the parts are belonging. Same as the different layers the parts from the same split look exactly the same be are not

Here is a naming convention : A_ where SPLIT_NUMBER has value 0 or 1.

This layer is quite simple, the worst is layer B ;)

Layer B

For this layer you will need both the box and the elastics ;)

The image below shows you what pieces of wood are wasted on this layer. All striped areas in red are the one that you actually can drop

Beware of the seat axle part, you must choose the right one, because if you don't choose the right one, you will not be able to put the rear axle.

Once all wasted wood parts are removed you directly write (no need to flip) on the different parts of the layer. Keep in mind the naming convention of layer A

Put all the tiny parts of the hinge into the box and keep them secure (try not to loose them)

Layer C

The image below shows you what pieces of wood are wasted on this layer. All striped areas in red are the one that you actually can drop

Once all wasted wood parts are removed you directly write (no need to flip) on the different parts of the layer. Keep in mind the naming convention of layer A

Mounting your chair

Before starting to mount it

Here is a list of tools you need

  • 300 ml of white wood glue (vinyl)
  • 40 little F Clamps
  • A set of wood pegs (8mm diameter)
  • About 35 clothespins (strong if possible) or clamp clips

First parts to glue

Hinge heads

You need to glue the tiny hinge crenel heads to the layer C. The hinge heads are parts of the B layer.

You need to glue them on both leg and seat and fasten them using the spins

The image below shows you what your chair in progress should look like

Wait at least 2 hours for a quick-setting glue

Slider axle

At the rear of the seat you have to glue the slider axle (provided by layer B) on the C part of the seat

Wait at least 2 hours for a quick-setting glue

Chair frame

The chair frame can be mounted in one pass even if you chose to cut a splited version of your chair

Advice: The peg holes are really adjusted to make the mounting operation easily. But I really advice to you to put them on layer A parts from the external to the internal face. Internal face is the one on which the part layer are written.

On layer A do not put glue on the bottom middle area of the frame. The area will not be fastened to another one

Put all your F Clamps on the frame as shown on the following image

Chair leg

You have to put glue on layer A (on the face on which you wrote)

Note: on the leg you have another slider for the leg axle. Do not put glue on it. Prefer making a dry run of the mount before putting glue (mark the glued areas). This slider is used to fold the chair. The slider length is about 10 cm, you have to put the C layer leg bottom in the right position. A detractor hole is there to help you by putting a peg into it you will have no mounting issue

Once you did not mistake you can glue the full leg

Do not forget to put glue on the hinge teeth and fasten them using clamps clips

Chair seat

This part is quite simple to mount

Do not forget to put glue on the hinge teeth and fasten them using clamps clips Do not forget to put glue on the seat rear slide axle and fasten them using clamps clips

Building the axles

Here is a list of stuff you need

  • 3 1m long strong 8mm threaded shafts
  • 4 8mm nuts
  • A bottle of thread locker
  • A pair of 13mm wide wrench

Seat slider

Cut a piece of threaded shaft at ${fullTopSliderAxleWidth}mm

Seat hinge

Cut a piece of threaded shaft at ${fullSeatHingeAxleWidth}mm

Seat leg axle

Cut a piece of threaded shaft at ${fullLegAxleWidth}mm
